Program details

Stability when housing is on the line

Door-to-Door Housing helps individuals, families, and veterans who are homeless or at imminent risk move from crisis to stable housing with fast support and a clear plan.

Each household is paired with case management to navigate next steps, remove barriers, and stay on track toward housing stability. Beyond direct assistance, Crossroads connects households to community services that support long-term stabilization and reduce the chance of future housing crises.

Frequently asked questions

Questions donors and families are likely to ask

Who does Door-to-Door Housing serve?

Door-to-Door Housing supports individuals, families, including children, and veterans who are experiencing homelessness or are at risk of becoming homeless.

What kind of help can someone receive?

The program provides emergency rent, utility, and move-in assistance, along with case management and community referrals.

Is this program free for people seeking help?

Yes. Door-to-Door Housing is offered as a support service for neighbors seeking stabilization. Formal eligibility details are confirmed during intake.

When is Door-to-Door Housing open?

The program is listed as open Monday to Friday, 9:30am to 3:30pm.

How do people apply?

Crossroads lists Door-to-Door Housing eligibility and application links on its website, including Mission United Family Assistance and Home Again pathways.

Does the program help veterans specifically?

Yes. Veterans are explicitly included among those served through Door-to-Door Housing.

What does move-in assistance usually mean?

Move-in assistance refers to costs that help someone move into housing, commonly deposits or fees. Exact covered items depend on each household’s situation.

How is the financial assistance paid out?

Payment methods vary by situation, for example direct payment to a landlord or utility provider. Crossroads confirms this during case management.

How can donors support someone beyond cash donations?

Crossroads also accepts gift-card donations so neighbors can celebrate their new homes with move-in gifts.

How do I know my donation is making a difference?

Crossroads publishes impact metrics such as households secured new housing, households avoiding eviction, and total households engaged, helping donors connect giving to concrete stabilization outcomes.
